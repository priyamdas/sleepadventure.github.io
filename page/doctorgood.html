Day 3 (Doctor Visit)

At the doctor's, you get a blood test done. The results show an astonishing deficit in white blood cell count.* Your doctor asks you whether you recently had any infections or illnesses that may have weakened your immune system. You reply that you've felt fine lately, just that work has been especially stressful this week and you haven't been sleeping too well. Your doctor surmises that a lack of sleep is behind the low numbers of natural killer cells -- your body's first line of defense against cancer -- and stresses the importance of getting enough sleep every day.Â 

That evening, you...
Head to bed early to give yourself the opportunity to get at least 9 hours of sleep.
Spend some time going over your notes for tomorrow's presentation before going to bed at a reasonable hour.
**Fun Fact: A 1994 study by Irwin et al. found that after just one night of 4 hours of sleep produced a 70% reduction of natural killer cells in the body.
